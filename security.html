Security & trust

Built to sit inside a regulated stack.

Clearway is the control plane — it does not hold your customers' funds or move money, and it is not a regulated entity. The bank and the fintech remain the obligated parties. Our job is to make their decisions fast, cited, and reproducible, while keeping their data isolated and every action logged.

Data

Isolated, encrypted, and never used to train models.

Encryption

Encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest under a customer-managed KMS key (database and secrets). Secrets resolve through a pluggable loader — AWS Secrets Manager, Vault, or mounted files — never embedded in the image.

Tenant isolation

Every row carries its tenant; data, catalog, audit, and the API keys that reach them are scoped per tenant. No cross-tenant access or leakage.

No model training

Customer data is used to render clearances, not to train models. Inference runs under zero-retention terms with our model provider.

Data minimization

We process the product and program configuration needed to clear — not end-customer PII beyond what an obligation requires.

Access & audit

Least privilege, and a log of everything.

Scoped API keys

Least-privilege scopes (clear / construct / ratify / admin), hashed at rest, with rotation and expiry. SSO/SAML and SCIM provisioning are on the roadmap.

Tamper-evident audit

Every clearance, ratification, and key event is appended to a sha-256 hash-chained ledger. A per-tenant head commitment can be published externally; interior edits and truncation are both detected, and the chain verifies on demand.

Reproducible

A determination replays from its cited controls and pinned catalog version — the artifact an examiner asks for — and the packet carries an attestation that the audit chain verified.

Human-in-the-loop

Controls enter the catalog only after a person ratifies them, gated on a recall check so coverage can't regress. The decision itself runs no model.

Application & infrastructure

Hardened where it actually gets attacked.

Safe outbound webhooks

Delivery is HMAC-signed and pinned to a re-validated public IP — an SSRF and DNS-rebinding guard blocks loopback, private, and cloud-metadata targets at both subscription and send time.

Abuse & DoS guards

Per-key rate limiting (distributed via Redis, degrading to in-process rather than failing open) and request-body size limits on every route.

Defined as code

Infrastructure is Terraform — private subnets, least-privilege security groups, managed Postgres, a TLS load balancer — deployed by CI/CD over GitHub OIDC with no static cloud keys.

Quality gate in CI

A recall-first eval over a hand-specified golden set runs on every change; a single missed obligation fails the build before it can reach a determination.

Subprocessors

A short, disclosed list.

We keep the subprocessor list small and current. A full, versioned list is provided under a signed agreement; the categories:

Model inference
LLM provider for the knowledge plane, under zero-retention / no-training terms.
Cloud infrastructure
Compute, storage, and managed databases in a major cloud region.
Observability
Error monitoring and tracing, scrubbed of customer content.
Clearway provides software tooling, not legal advice, and is not a bank, money transmitter, or regulated entity. Determinations support a customer's own compliance program; they do not replace it.
Compliance posture

Honest about where we are.

SOC 2

SOC 2 Type II is on our roadmap. The architecture — least privilege, append-only audit, reproducibility — is built for it from day one.

Examination support

Because every clearance is reproducible from cited controls and a pinned catalog version, customers can answer "how did this clear?" with evidence.

Change management

Catalog and policy changes are versioned, reviewed, and gated by a recall-first eval before they can affect a determination.

Data residency

Region pinning is available for customers with residency requirements.

Responsible disclosure

We welcome good-faith security research and will not pursue researchers who act in good faith, avoid privacy violations and service degradation, and give us reasonable time to remediate.
