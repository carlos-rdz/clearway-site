Company

The authorization layer for shipping regulated products.

Every regulated product launch re-litigates the same body of law by hand, through a chain of reviewers, every single time. We think that's backwards. The law is shared; the review shouldn't be repeated. Clearway encodes the law once, clears what's already covered deterministically, and sends a person only the part that's genuinely new.

Why we exist

The work should disappear — not the accountability.

A new product has to satisfy the rules that apply to it: identity, disclosures, fair lending, sanctions, money transmission, model risk, and dozens more. Those rules don't change between products. Yet most organizations re-derive the answer from scratch on every launch — months of serial review across Legal, Compliance, Risk, and committee.

Clearway turns that body of law into a versioned, cited, machine-checkable catalog of controls. A product is decomposed and matched against it. What's covered clears in seconds, with a citation. What's new is routed to the accountable function — and once reviewed, it becomes a control that clears every product after it. The accountable signature stays where it belongs. The repetitive analyst work goes away.

How we work

Principles we don't bend.

Deterministic where it counts

The clearance decision runs no model. It is matching and policy-as-code, so it can't drift or hallucinate a result.

Cited or it doesn't ship

Every control and obligation carries its clause. There is no field for an uncited compliance claim.

Human on the exceptions

Agents do the analyst work; people ratify novel controls and own the sign-off. Recorded, append-only.

Recall before precision

A missed obligation is the only unacceptable error. We'd rather flag than miss, and the eval gate enforces it.

Who's behind it

Solo-founded, and early.

Clearway Agents is a solo startup — one founder building the catalog model, the clearance engine, and the first conversations with design partners. What you see here is the product and the thesis, in the open. No team that doesn't exist yet, no logos that aren't real.
