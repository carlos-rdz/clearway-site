Product

From a product spec to cleared — with a citation on every determination.

A team describes what they're shipping. Clearway decomposes it into components, finds the obligations that govern each one, matches them to approved controls, and renders a decision. Components already covered clear automatically; anything genuinely new is routed to the accountable function — and once reviewed, it becomes a control that clears every product after it.

The lifecycle

One pass, six steps, fully recorded.

1 · Intake

The product or change is decomposed into discrete components — onboarding, disclosures, fees, data handling, a funds-movement path, a model — each with its jurisdictions.

2 · Retrieve

For each component, the applicable obligations are pulled from a versioned catalog of the source law — at clause granularity, not document granularity.

3 · Match

Each component is matched against an approved control that already satisfies its obligation, with the control's parameters checked against the product's.

4 · Decide

Policy-as-code evaluates the match. Covered components clear; a component with no approved control — or one outside a control's parameters — becomes a delta.

5 · Route

Deltas go to the accountable function (Compliance, Legal, Model Risk…), narrowed to exactly what's new. Everything else is already cleared.

6 · Record

The determination, its cited controls, and the catalog version are appended to a tamper-evident, hash-chained ledger — and packaged as an examiner packet that reproduces the decision exactly, later.

The agents

A small team of roles, each with one job.

The agents do the analyst work that today consumes the review cycle. They operate on the knowledge base and the routing — never on the live decision itself, which stays deterministic.

Intake

Decomposes a product into components and identifies the jurisdictions and data each one touches.

Research

Retrieves the controlling obligations for each component from the catalog, at clause granularity.

Controls

Matches each component to an approved control and checks the product's parameters against the control's.

Compliance

Applies policy-as-code; flags any component with no approved control or one outside its blessed range.

Risk

Triages deltas — drafts the control that would cover a novel component and routes it to the accountable function.

Record

Writes the determination, citations, and catalog version to the append-only audit log.

The flywheel

A control is reviewed once, then applies to every later product.

The escape hatch — routing a novel component to a person — is also how the system gets stronger. Each ratified control widens the set of products that clear automatically.

What clears today

Four product types, end to end — every obligation cited to real law.

These aren't mockups. Each clears against a catalog built from the source CFR/USC, with the determination reproducible from its pinned version.

Credit card · US

CIP, OFAC, Reg Z disclosures, ECOA/Reg B adverse action, FCRA, MLA, GLBA safeguards.

BNPL · US

Reg Z applicability via finance-charge or >4-installment triggers, plus MLA/SCRA where they attach.

Money movement · US (MSB)

FinCEN MSB rules, the Travel Rule, remittance transfers (Reg E §1005.30), and prepaid/stored-value.

Deposit account · US

Reg DD, Reg CC, Reg E including overdraft opt-in, and FDIC Part 328 signage/advertising.

The same engine takes a new product type by adding its catalog slice — built by the agents, ratified by a person, recall-checked before it can clear anything.
