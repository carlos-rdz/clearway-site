Architecture

A deterministic decision over an agent-built knowledge base.

Two planes, deliberately separated. The clearance decision is deterministic and replayable — it runs no model. The agents work on the knowledge base behind it: ingesting the source law, extracting cited controls, and keeping them current. The split is the point — it is what lets a bank and an examiner trust an automated determination.

The two planes

Where the model runs, and where it deliberately doesn't.

decision plane · the hot path
  • Component → control matching plus policy-as-code (OPA/Rego-style). No model in the loop.
  • Sub-second and deterministic; the same inputs always produce the same determination.
  • Every result carries its controlling clause and the control that satisfied it.
  • Tamper-evident, hash-chained ledger — each clearance replays from cited inputs plus the pinned catalog version, and the chain verifies on demand.
knowledge plane · agentic
  • Ingest source law → clause-level chunking → structured extraction with forced tool-use, so a citation is mandatory at the schema layer.
  • Proposed controls are human-ratified before they enter the catalog, and versioned.
  • A change-feed re-flags controls affected by amendments; a product cannot clear against a superseded control.
  • Tiered models — a strong model for synthesis and triage, a cheap model for extraction and classification; system prompt and corpus are prompt-cached.
The stack

Six components, each replaceable except the middle.

retrieval
Clause-level chunks with metadata (regulation, section, jurisdiction, version). Hybrid vector + BM25 with a reranker, over a versioned corpus.
policy engine
Policy-as-code (OPA/Rego-style). Versioned and unit-tested per obligation, so a rule change is a reviewable diff.
models
Tiered by job — synthesis vs. extraction. Forced-tool-use schemas make structured, cited output mandatory. System + corpus prompt-cached.
audit
Tamper-evident, sha-256 hash-chained ledger with an externally anchorable per-tenant head. Every determination pins its catalog version, so it is reproducible — and provably unaltered — for examination.
integration
MCP surface — exposes a clear() tool and consumes one connector per upstream system (rails, KYC, ticketing).
quality gate
Recall-first eval harness. A missed obligation is the catastrophic error, so CI blocks a deploy that regresses recall.
Data model

Everything is a cited object.

A product is a composition of components; each determination references the control and clause that produced it. There is no field for an uncited compliance claim.

ProductSpec
name, description, jurisdictions, and a list of components.
Component
a discrete part of the product (onboarding, fees, a model, a funds path) with its parameters.
Obligation
a controlling rule retrieved from the catalog, at clause granularity, with a citation.
Control
an approved, parameterized way of satisfying one or more obligations; human-ratified and versioned.
Determination
cleared or routed, with the matched control, the cited clause, and the catalog version.
Citation
{ regulation, clause, url, catalog_version } — mandatory on every Obligation and Control.

Trust properties

Why a determination holds up.

Deterministic

The decision is matching plus policy evaluation. No model runs in it, so it cannot drift or hallucinate a clearance.

Cited

Every cleared component names its clause and control. Every routed component names what's missing.

Reproducible

Pin the catalog version and prompt versions and a past clearance replays exactly — the artifact an examiner asks for.

Recall-first

The eval gate optimizes recall: an unmatched obligation is surfaced, never silently skipped. A regression blocks deploy.
