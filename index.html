Automated regulatory clearance

Every product and change is checked against the regulations that apply — before it ships. Clearway runs that check.

A product has to satisfy the rules that apply to it — identity (31 CFR 1020.220), disclosures (Reg Z §1026.6), fair lending (ECOA §1002.9), sanctions (OFAC), money transmission per state, and more. The rules don't change between products. Clearway decomposes a product into its parts, maps each to its controlling obligation and an approved control, clears what's already covered, and routes only the genuinely new to a person.

Inside a clearance

The real flow: a deterministic decision, with agents only where it's genuinely new.

This mirrors the engine. The decision plane is deterministic — decompose, test applicability, look up the approved control, decide, record — no model in the loop, every check cited to real CFR/USC. Only when an obligation has no control does the knowledge plane kick in: agents draft a control, a verifier checks it, and a human ratifies it before it can ever clear anything. Catalog construction

A control is reviewed once, then applies to every later product.

When a component has no matching control, the accountable function reviews it and the control is added to the catalog, cited to its clause. The next product with that component matches the existing control.

Under the hood

A deterministic decision over an agent-built knowledge base.

Two planes. The clearance decision is deterministic and replayable; the agents work on the knowledge base behind it, never on the live decision. Full architecture →

decision plane · the hot path
  • Component → control matching plus policy-as-code (OPA/Rego-style) — no model in the loop
  • Every result carries its clause; sub-second and deterministic
  • Append-only event log — each clearance replays from cited inputs + catalog version
knowledge plane · agentic
  • Ingest source law → clause-level chunking → structured extraction with forced tool-use (citations mandatory)
  • Controls are human-ratified and versioned; a change-feed re-flags affected controls
  • Tiered models for synthesis vs. extraction; system + corpus prompt-cached
